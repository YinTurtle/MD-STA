### Metabolism
The mechanisms that **couple constant energy demand** with **intermittent fuel supply** (you don’t eat constantly, but cells need energy constantly).

Can be divided into 2 types:
#### Anabolism
**Synthesis** of new molecules to **store energy** and build structures (e.g., glycogen, fat, protein)
#### Catabolism
**Degradation/breakdown** of molecules to **release energy** (and produce smaller building blocks)
#### ATP (adenosine triphosphate)
Energy currency

### Oxidation & Reduction
oxidation + reduction = redox
#### Oxidation
Gain O₂ **or** loss of H⁺ **or** loss of electrons (e⁻)
#### Reduction
Loss O₂ **or** gain of H⁺ **or** gain of electrons (e⁻)
>[!info] Mnemonic
>**OILRIG** = Oxidation Is Loss (of electrons), Reduction Is Gain

#### Redox Reaction
Many steps of metabolic pathways are reversible redox reactions.
**2** important enzymes involved in metabolism:
- **Nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide (NAD+ )** – derived from *Vit B3/Niacin*
- **Flavin adenine dinucleotide (FAD)** – derived from *Vit B2/riboflavin*
Both NAD+ and FAD are acceptors of H+ ions/electrons (e-)

### Major fuels of metabolism
Carbohydrates, fats, proteins

#### 3 stages of metabolism
>**Stage 1**
Digestion + absorption + transport in blood
>**Stage 2**
In the cell cytoplasm:
>- Anabolic - nutrients built into storage molecules (e.g. glycogen/protein/adipose)
>- Catabolic - nutrients/storage molecules broken down to **pyruvate** or **acetyl-CoA**.
>**Stage 3**
In the mitochondria:
Catabolism requiring **oxygen** to completely break down food and generate **ATP**

### ATP
ATP is the essential link between catabolism and anabolism:
- Catabolism **captures energy** into ATP
- ATP is then **spent** to drive energy-requiring processes (active transport, contraction, synthesis, growth)
- ATP, ADP & AMP are interconvertible
	- GTP (guanosine triphosphate) and CP (creatine phosphate) can serve similar roles

#### Why does it work well?
- ATP stores energy in **phosphate bonds** (phosphoanhydride bonds).
- When ATP is hydrolysed: **ATP → ADP + Pi**, about **−30.5 kJ/mol** is released

> [!info] First law of thermodynamics
> Total energy in the universe is constant, energy can neither be created nor destroy.
