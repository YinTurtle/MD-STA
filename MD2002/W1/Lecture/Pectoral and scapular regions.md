### Anterior chest (Pectoral region)
#### Subclavius
- **Origin:** Junction of 1st rib and its costal cartilage
- **Insertion:** Inferior surface of middle third of clavicle (subclavian groove)
- **Main actions:** Stabilises and depresses clavicle at the SC joint; helps protect underlying neurovascular structures by “splinting” the clavicle
- **Nerve:** Nerve to subclavius (C5–C6)
#### Pectoralis major
- **Origin:**
    - Clavicular head: medial half of clavicle
    - Sternocostal head: sternum + costal cartilages (upper ribs)
    - (Often also from external oblique aponeurosis)
- **Insertion:** Lateral lip of intertubercular sulcus (bicipital groove) of humerus
- **Main actions (GH joint):** **Adduction** and **medial rotation** of humerus; assists **flexion** (clavicular head) and **extension from flexed position** (sternocostal head)
- **Nerve:** Lateral pectoral nerve (C5–C7) and medial pectoral nerve (C8–T1)
#### Pectoralis minor
- **Origin:** Ribs 3–5 near their costal cartilages
- **Insertion:** Coracoid process of scapula (medial/superior surface)
- **Main actions (scapula):** **Stabilises** scapula against thoracic wall; **depresses** and assists **protraction**; contributes to downward rotation depending on scapular position
- **Nerve:** Medial pectoral nerve (C8–T1)
#### Serratus anterior
- **Origin:** External surfaces of ribs 1–8 (sometimes 1–9)
- **Insertion:** Anterior surface of medial border of scapula (especially inferior angle)
- **Main actions (scapula):** **Protraction**; **holds scapula to thoracic wall** (prevents winging); key for **upward rotation** to allow arm elevation above 90°
- **Nerve:** Long thoracic nerve (C5–C7)
### Extrinsic back muscles (scapula/humerus)
#### Trapezius
- **Origin:** External occipital protuberance, medial superior nuchal line, ligamentum nuchae, spinous processes C7–T12
- **Insertion:** Lateral third clavicle, acromion, spine of scapula
- **Main actions (scapula):**
    - Upper fibres: **elevation** + assists **upward rotation**
    - Middle fibres: **retraction**
    - Lower fibres: **depression** + assists **upward rotation**  
        (Upper + lower fibres work with serratus anterior for upward rotation.)
- **Nerve:** **Spinal accessory nerve (CN XI)** (motor) with sensory/proprioceptive input via **C3–C4**
#### Latissimus dorsi
- **Origin:** Spinous processes T7–T12, thoracolumbar fascia, iliac crest, inferior ribs (often 9–12)
- **Insertion:** Floor of intertubercular sulcus of humerus
- **Main actions (GH joint):** **Extension**, **adduction**, **medial rotation** of humerus
- **Nerve:** Thoracodorsal nerve (C6–C8)
#### Rhomboid minor
- **Origin:** Nuchal ligament, spinous processes C7–T1
- **Insertion:** Medial end (root) of scapular spine
- **Main actions (scapula):** **Retraction**, **downward rotation**, stabilises scapula on thoracic wall
- **Nerve:** Dorsal scapular nerve (C4–C5)
#### Rhomboid major
- **Origin:** Spinous processes T2–T5
- **Insertion:** Medial border of scapula (from spine to inferior angle)
- **Main actions (scapula):** **Retraction**, **downward rotation**, stabilisation
- **Nerve:** Dorsal scapular nerve (C4–C5)
#### Levator scapulae
- **Origin:** Transverse processes C1–C4
- **Insertion:** Superior part of medial border of scapula (above spine; near superior angle)
- **Main actions (scapula):** **Elevation**, assists **downward rotation** (tilts glenoid inferiorly); helps stabilise scapula
- **Nerve:** Cervical nerves (C3–C4) and dorsal scapular nerve (C4–C5)
### Intrinsic shoulder muscles (scapula->humerus; move GH joint)
#### Deltoid
- **Origin:** Lateral third clavicle (anterior), acromion (middle), spine of scapula (posterior)
- **Insertion:** Deltoid tuberosity of humerus
- **Main actions (GH joint):**
    - Anterior (clavicular): **flexion** + **medial rotation**
    - Middle (acromial): **abduction** (major abductor after initial 15°)
    - Posterior (spinal): **extension** + **lateral rotation**
- **Nerve:** Axillary nerve (C5–C6)
#### Teres major
- **Origin:** Posterior surface of inferior angle of scapula
- **Insertion:** Medial lip of intertubercular sulcus (crest of lesser tubercle) of humerus
- **Main actions (GH joint):** **Adduction** + **medial rotation** (also assists extension from flexed position)
- **Nerve:** Lower subscapular nerve (C5–C6)
### Rotator cuffs (S.I.T.S)
The cuff muscles **surround the humeral head** and **hold it in the glenoid** during movement; **3 insert on the greater tuberosity, 1 on the lesser tuberosity**.
#### Supraspinatus
- **Origin:** Supraspinous fossa of scapula
- **Insertion:** **Greater tuberosity** (superior facet) of humerus
- **Main actions:** **Initiates abduction (first ~15°)**; compresses humeral head into glenoid for stability
- **Nerve:** Suprascapular nerve (C4–C6)
#### Infraspinatus
- **Origin:** Infraspinous fossa of scapula
- **Insertion:** **Greater tuberosity** (middle facet) of humerus
- **Main actions:** **External (lateral) rotation**; GH stabilisation
- **Nerve:** Suprascapular nerve (C5–C6)
#### Teres minor
- **Origin:** Middle part of lateral border of scapula
- **Insertion:** **Greater tuberosity** (inferior facet) of humerus
- **Main actions:** **External (lateral) rotation** (also weak adduction); GH stabilisation
- **Nerve:** Axillary nerve (C5–C6)
#### Subscapularis
- **Origin:** Subscapular fossa (anterior surface of scapula)
- **Insertion:** **Lesser tuberosity** of humerus
- **Main actions:** **Internal (medial) rotation** (also adduction); GH stabilisation
- **Nerve:** Upper and lower subscapular nerves (C5–C7)
### Fascia
#### Clavipectoral fascia
Associated with **pectoralis minor** region (a fascial layer deep to pectoralis major)
#### Pectoral fascia
Covers pectoralis major and contributes to the connective tissue organisation of anterior chest wall
#### Axillary fascia
Forms the **base (floor) of the axilla**; is continuous with pectoral fascia anteriorly and other fascial layers
#### Brachial fascia
Described as acting like a **“sock”** around the arm; provides containment and structural organisation for arm compartments
#### Supraspinatus/Infraspinatus fascia
Dense fascia over those fossae; contributes to the **muscle origins** there (important for understanding why those muscles have broad origins)
### Movement
#### Scapula
- **Protraction:** *serratus anterior (prime)*, pectoralis minor
- **Retraction:** *middle trapezius (prime)*, rhomboids
- **Elevation:** *upper trapezius*, levator scapulae
- **Depression:** *gravity*, lower trapezius, pectoralis minor
- **Upward rotation:** *upper* + lower *trapezius* with *serratus anterior* (key force couple)
- **Downward rotation:** *latissimus dorsi*, rhomboids, levator scapulae, pectoralis minor (with assistance depending on position)
#### Glenohumeral joint
- **Abduction (initiation):** supraspinatus; **abduction (main):** deltoid
- **External rotation:** infraspinatus, teres minor
- **Internal rotation/adduction:** subscapularis, pectoralis major, latissimus dorsi, teres major
### Axilla
- Fat filled space at the junction of upper limb and trunk, providing a route for major neurovascular structures
- Continuous superiorly with the neck via the cervico-axillary canal
#### Walls (4) & floor (1)
##### Anterior wall
pectoral region muscles (pectoralis major/minor region)
##### Posterior wall
subscapularis/teres major/latissimus region
##### Medial wall
thoracic wall + serratus anterior
##### Lateral wall
humerus/intertubercular region (a narrow bony wall)
##### Floor (base)
**axillary fascia** + skin
#### Cervico-axillary canal
The opening between neck and axilla allowing passage of the neurovascular bundle into the limb
#### Contents of the axilla
- Axillary artery and vein
- Cords/branches of the brachial plexus
- Axillary lymph nodes
- The **axillary sheath** (fascial sleeve enclosing axillary vessels and brachial plexus)
#### Axillary Lymph Nodes (5)
- **Anterior (pectoral)**
- **Posterior (subscapular)**
- **Lateral (humeral)**
- **Central**
- **Apical**
