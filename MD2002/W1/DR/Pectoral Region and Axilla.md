#### Cephalic Vein
The cephalic vein is a superficial vein of the upper limb that forms from the dorsal venous network of the hand, ascends on the lateral aspect of the forearm and arm and drains into the axillary vein.

#### Axillary Lymph Nodes
Axillary lymph nodes is a group of lymphoid tissue that drain the lymph from the upper limb, lateral quadrants of the breast, thoracic wall and the anterior abdominal wall above the level of the umbilicus. The nodes are arranged in 5 groups: Anterior, posterior, lateral, central and apical. However, surgically the nodes are arranged in 3 levels: Level I, II and III; inferior, posterior and superior (medial) to pectoralis minor, respectively
