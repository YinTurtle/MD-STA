## üß± **1. Overview of the Vertebral Column**

- Consists of **33 vertebrae** divided as:
    
    - 7 **Cervical**
        
    - 12 **Thoracic**
        
    - 5 **Lumbar**
        
    - 5 **Sacral** (fused ‚Üí sacrum)
        
    - 1‚Äì4 **Coccygeal** (fused ‚Üí coccyx)
        

### **Functions**

- Supports **body weight** and **upright posture**.
    
- Protects and transmits **spinal cord and nerves**.
    
- Provides **attachment** for muscles of the trunk and limbs.
    
- Supports the **skull** and contributes to **thoracic cage** and **locomotion (movement - as it is attached to the muscles)**.

## üß© **3. Typical Vertebra ‚Äì Key Features**

- **Vertebral body** ‚Äì weight bearing.
    
- **Vertebral arch** = pedicles + laminae.
    
- **Spinous process** ‚Äì posterior projection.
    
- **Transverse processes** ‚Äì lateral projections.
    
- **Articular processes** ‚Äì form **facet joints** (synovial).
    
- **Vertebral foramen** ‚Äì forms the **vertebral canal** for the spinal cord.
    
- **Superior and inferior vertebral notches** form the **intervertebral foramina** (for spinal nerves).
    

**Clinical Note:**  
Facet joints are prone to **arthritis**, causing **back pain**.

## ü¶¥ **4. Regional Vertebral Features**

### **Cervical Vertebrae (C3‚ÄìC7)**

- **Small bodies**, **large vertebral foramina**.
    
- **Bifid spinous process** (except C7).
    
- **Transverse foramina** (for vertebral artery & vein).
    
- **Uncinate processes** form **Luschka‚Äôs joints (uncovertebral joints)** with vertebra above.
    
![[Pasted image 20251111003348.png]]

**Atypical Cervical Vertebrae:**

- **C1 (Atlas):** no vertebral body, anterior & posterior arches connecting lateral masses, articulates with occipital condyles (superior articular facets).
![[Pasted image 20251111003618.png]]
    
- **C2 (Axis):** odontoid process (dens) ‚Üí rotation pivot for atlas - head and neck at the atlanto-axial joint.
![[Pasted image 20251111003755.png]]
    
- **C7 (Vertebra prominens):** long, non-bifid spine (palpable landmark).
    

---

### **Thoracic Vertebrae (T1‚ÄìT12)**

- **Heart-shaped body**, **circular foramen**.
    
- **Costal demi-facets** on body (rib heads) + **costal facets** on transverse processes (tubercle of rib).
    
- **Spinous processes** long, sloping inferiorly.
    
- **Facets oriented for rotation**, but flexion/extension limited.
    
![[Pasted image 20251111012400.png]]

---
### **Lumbar Vertebrae (L1‚ÄìL5)**

- **Largest body**.
    
- **Short. sturdy, stumpy, thick spinous processes** (quadrangular).
    
- **Superior articular facets** face **postero-medially** (resist rotation).
    
- Allow **flexion, extension, and lateral flexion** (no rotation).
    
![[Pasted image 20251111012635.png|200]]
---

### **Sacrum & Coccyx**

- **Sacrum:** 5 fused vertebrae, connects spine to pelvis.
    
    - **Sacral promontory** ‚Äì obstetric (important for females) landmark. - S1
        
    - **Sacral canal** ‚Äì continuation of vertebral canal (contains **cauda equina**).
        
    - **Sacral hiatus** ‚Äì site for **caudal epidural anaesthesia**.
        
    - **Foramina:** transmit anterior & posterior rami of spinal nerves.
        
- **Coccyx:** 3‚Äì4 fused vertebrae, vestigial tailbone.
    
![[Pasted image 20251111012905.png]]

## üß´ **6. Intervertebral Disc**

- 20-25% of **length** of the **column**
- **Annulus fibrosus:** concentric fibrocartilage rings for strength. Attached and kept in place by anterior and posterior longitudinal ligaments.
    
- **Nucleus pulposus:** gelatinous centre (70‚Äì90 % water) ‚Üí shock absorber.
    
- **Supplied by:**
    
    - **Peripheral capillaries** (outer disc)
        
    - **Diffusion** from vertebral bodies (inner disc)
        
![[Pasted image 20251111013233.png]]
**Function:**  
Allows **movement**, distributes **compressive forces**, and maintains **spinal curvature**.

- Periphery (border) of the disc is supplied by neighbouring capillaries
- Centre is nourished by diffusion from the body of the vertebra
![[Pasted image 20251111013424.png]]
- Discs may deform to allow movement between the vertebral bodies
- This may put pressure on the **nucleus pulposus**
- The **annulus fibrosus** may degenerate and weaken
![[Pasted image 20251111013625.png]]

**Clinical ‚Äì Herniated (‚Äúslipped‚Äù) disc:**

- Degeneration ‚Üí nucleus pulposus herniates **postero-laterally** (weak point).
    
- Compresses spinal nerve ‚Üí **radicular pain**.
    
- Most common at **L4/5 ‚Üí L5 root**, or **L5/S1 ‚Üí S1 root (cauda equina - beginning of the 'horse tail'**.
    
![[Pasted image 20251111013925.png|400]]

## ü™¢ **7. Ligaments of the Vertebral Column**

| Ligament                            | Location                                                                                                                        | Function                                                                  |
| ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Anterior longitudinal ligament**  | Along anterior surfaces of vertebral bodies. **Anterior** surfaces of the vertebral bodies between **Atlas** to the **Sacrum**. | Limits **extension**                                                      |
| **Posterior longitudinal ligament** | Within vertebral canal, posterior to bodies. **Posterior** surface of bodies of vertebrae between **C2** to **sacrum**          | Limits **hyper-flexion**                                                  |
| **Ligamentum flavum**               | Between laminae                                                                                                                 | Elastic, resists and limit sudden flexion, aids return to upright posture |
| **Interspinous ligament**           | Between spinous processes                                                                                                       | Limits flexion                                                            |
| **Supraspinous ligament**           | Tips of spinous processes (between C7‚Äìsacrum)                                                                                   | Limits flexion                                                            |
| **Ligamentum nuchae**               | External occipital protuberance ‚Üí C7                                                                                            | Supports head, muscle attachment, limit flexion                           |
| **Intertransverse ligaments**       | Between transverse processes                                                                                                    | Limit lateral flexion                                                     |
![[Pasted image 20251111014403.png]]
![[Pasted image 20251111014545.png]]
**Special Craniovertebral Ligaments:**

- **Alar ligament:** limits rotation of the head.
    
- **Transverse ligament of atlas:** holds dens against anterior arch (C1‚ÄìC2 stability).
    
- **Tectorial membrane:** continuation of posterior longitudinal ligament. Extends between the foramen magnum and C2.
    
- **Atlanto-occipital / Atlanto-axial membranes:** continuation of anterior longitudinal ligament.
    

**Clinical:**

- Whiplash ‚Üí strain of interspinous & supraspinous ligaments.

## ‚ù§Ô∏è **8. Vascular Supply** 

- **Arteries:** from vertebral, intercostal, and lumbar arteries ‚Üí form **anastomotic rings**.
    
- **Veins:**
    
    - **Internal vertebral venous plexus (in canal)**.
        
    - **External vertebral venous plexus (outside vertebrae)**.
        
    - **Basivertebral veins (within vertebral body)**.
        
![[Pasted image 20251111014647.png]]

ü©∏ **Clinical:**  
Vertebral venous plexus **lacks valves** ‚Üí allows **tumour metastasis** (e.g., prostate ‚Üí spine).

## üí™ **9. Muscles of the Back** 

### **Classification**

| Type                                                                       | Origin (embryological) | Function                                                                                                                                                                                  | Innervation                         |
| -------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| **Extrinsic** (superficial)                                                | Hypaxial myotome       | Move upper limb & thoracic wall                                                                                                                                                           | **Anterior rami** of spinal nerves  |
| **Intrinsic** (deep - between spinous processes and the angle of the ribs) | Epaxial myotome        | Move/stabilise vertebral column. Adapted to provide support and movement in resistance to the effect of gravity - stronger than the flexors because extension is in opposition to gravity | **Posterior rami** of spinal nerves |

---

Mainly for upper limb movement.

|       | **Latissimus dorsi** (iliac crest, fascia, spines of T6 to T12, lower ribs; to humerus) | Extends, adducts, internally rotates shoulder                   | **Thoracodorsal nerve**            |

|       | **Serratus posterior superior**                                                         | Assists inspiration                                             | Intercostal nerves                 |
|       | **Serratus posterior inferior**                                                         | Assists expiration                                              | Intercostal nerves                 |

### **Intrinsic (Deep) Muscles**

- From **skull**, through **cervical, thoracic**, and **lumbar** regions to **pelvis**
- Maintain **posture** (keeping vertebral column upright) and **extend spine** against gravity.
    
- Arranged in **three layers** (superficial, intermediate, deep).
    

#### **Superficial Intrinsic**

- **Splenius capitis & cervicis**
    
    - Bilateral: extension of head/neck. (acting together)
        
    - Unilateral: lateral flexion & rotation. (acting alone)
        
![[Pasted image 20251111015617.png|400]]

**Mnemonic:** _I Love Spaghetti_

|Muscle|Origin ‚Üí Insertion|Function|
|---|---|---|
|**Iliocostalis**|Ilium ‚Üí ribs|Lateral flexion, extension|
|**Longissimus**|Transverse processes|Extension & rotation|
|**Spinalis**|Spinous processes|Extension|
![[Pasted image 20251111015716.png]]

| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------ |
| **Transversospinales** | Semispinalis (capitis, cervicis, thoracis), Multifidus, Rotatores        | Stabilise, rotate, extend vertebrae              |
| **Suboccipital group** | Rectus capitis posterior major/minor, Obliquus capitis superior/inferior | Extend & rotate head; form suboccipital triangle |

## ‚öïÔ∏è **10. Clinical Correlations**

|Condition|Cause|Effect|
|---|---|---|
|**Herniated disc**|Degeneration of annulus fibrosus|Nerve root compression ‚Üí sciatica|
|**Whiplash**|Rapid flexion-extension|Sprain of cervical ligaments|
|**Scoliosis**|Lateral curvature|Asymmetry in vertebral growth|
|**Spondylolisthesis**|Slippage of vertebral body|Compression of spinal nerves|
|**Spinal stenosis**|Narrowing of canal/foramina|Back pain, limb weakness|
|**Metastasis**|Via vertebral venous plexus|Secondary tumours in spine|
