## **1. Overview of the Nervous System**

**Divisions:**

- **Central Nervous System (CNS):** brain + spinal cord
    
- **Peripheral Nervous System (PNS):** cranial and spinal nerves + ganglia
    

**Functional divisions:**

- **Sensory (afferent):** carries signals _to_ CNS.
    
- **Motor (efferent):** carries commands _from_ CNS ‚Üí effectors.
    
    - **Somatic motor:** skeletal muscle (voluntary).
        
    - **Autonomic (visceral):** smooth & cardiac muscle, glands (involuntary).
        
        - **Sympathetic:** fight-or-flight.
            
        - **Parasympathetic:** rest-and-digest.
            

---

## **2. Neurons**

- **Functional unit** of the nervous system.
    
- Composed of:
    
    - **Cell body (soma)**
        
    - **Dendrites** ‚Äì short, branched receptive extensions.
        
    - **Axon** ‚Äì single, long conducting fibre (up to 1 m).
        

**Properties:**

- Excitable ‚Üí generate **action potentials**.
    
- Communicate via **synapses** (axodendritic/axoaxonal).
    
- Use **neurotransmitters** for signal transmission.
    

**Exam Focus:**  
Label neuron diagram: soma, dendrites, axon, myelin sheath, synapse.

---

## **3. Neuroglia (Glial Cells)**

- Support and protect neurons; **outnumber neurons**.
    
- Can **regenerate**, unlike most neurons.
    
- Types:
    
    - **CNS:** astrocytes, oligodendrocytes, microglia, ependymal cells.
        
    - **PNS:** Schwann cells, satellite cells.
        

---

## **4. Myelin and Schwann Sheath**

- **Myelin:** lipid-rich insulating layer around axons.
    
- **Produced by:**
    
    - **Schwann cells** in the **PNS**.
        
    - **Oligodendrocytes** in the **CNS**.
        
- Increases **conduction velocity** and reduces energy cost.
    

**Clinical Focus:**  
Loss of myelin ‚Üí demyelinating diseases like **multiple sclerosis (CNS)** and **Guillain-Barr√© syndrome (PNS)**.

---

## **5. Gray and White Matter**

|Feature|Gray Matter|White Matter|
|---|---|---|
|Composition|Neuronal **cell bodies**, dendrites, unmyelinated axons|**Myelinated axons**|
|CNS location|Cortex (surface of cerebrum/cerebellum) + nuclei deep in brain|Internal to gray matter in brain; surrounds gray matter in spinal cord|
|PNS term|**Ganglion**|**Nerve**|

**Exam Focus:**  
Know location of gray vs white matter in _brain_ vs _spinal cord_ (inverted pattern).

## **6. Central Nervous System Components**

**CNS includes:**

1. **Spinal Cord**
    
2. **Brainstem** ‚Äì midbrain, pons, medulla
    
3. **Cerebellum**
    
4. **Cerebrum (Telencephalon + Diencephalon)**
    

üß© _Encephalon_ = brainstem + cerebellum + cerebrum

## **7. Cerebrum**

- Two hemispheres separated by **longitudinal fissure**.
    
- Surface folded into **gyri** (ridges) separated by **sulci** (grooves).
    
- Each hemisphere = **cerebral cortex (gray)** + **white matter** + **basal nuclei**.
    

**Cerebral lobes & functions:**

|Lobe|Key Functions|
|---|---|
|**Frontal**|Motor control, speech, planning, personality|
|**Parietal**|Somatosensory processing|
|**Temporal**|Auditory, memory, emotion|
|**Occipital**|Vision|
|**Limbic**|Emotion, motivation, memory|
|**Insula**|Taste, visceral sensation|

**Lateralisation:**

- **Left hemisphere:** language, logic, math.
    
- **Right hemisphere:** creativity, spatial ability.
    

**Clinical Focus:**

- **Stroke** in left hemisphere ‚Üí aphasia.
    
- **Frontal lobe damage** ‚Üí personality changes.
    

**Exam Focus:**  
Be able to **label lobes** and **match functions**.

---

## **8. Diencephalon**

|Structure|Function|
|---|---|
|**Thalamus**|Relay station for sensory input & motor coordination; encloses 3rd ventricle|
|**Hypothalamus**|Autonomic + endocrine control; regulates temperature, thirst, hunger, sleep, sexual behaviour|

**Clinical Focus:**  
Lesions in hypothalamus ‚Üí endocrine dysfunction, thermoregulation issues.

---

## **9. Brainstem**

|Region|Features / Functions|
|---|---|
|**Midbrain**|Cranial nerve nuclei (III‚ÄìIV); visual & auditory reflexes|
|**Pons**|Cranial nerve nuclei (V‚ÄìVIII); links cerebrum ‚Üî cerebellum; regulates respiration|
|**Medulla oblongata**|Vital centres: cardiac, respiratory, vasomotor; pyramidal decussation; reflexes (cough, sneeze, swallow, vomit)|

**Exam Focus:**  
Identify **pyramidal decussation** (motor crossing) in medulla.

---

## **10. Cerebellum**

- Two hemispheres in posterior cranial fossa.
    
- **Gray cortex** outside, **white matter + deep nuclei** inside.
    
- Coordinates **balance**, **posture**, and **smooth movements**.
    

**Clinical Focus:**  
Cerebellar damage ‚Üí **ataxia**, dysmetria, intention tremor.

---

## **11. Spinal Cord**

- Two-way conduction pathway and **reflex centre**.
    
- Extends from **foramen magnum ‚Üí L1/L2** vertebral level.
    
- Protected by **vertebrae, meninges, CSF**.
    

### **External features**

- **Cervical & lumbosacral enlargements.**
    
- **Conus medullaris** = tapered end ‚Üí **cauda equina** (nerve roots).
    

### **Cross-sectional structure**

|Gray matter|White matter|
|---|---|
|Dorsal horn ‚Üí sensory interneurons|Ascending & descending tracts|
|Lateral horn ‚Üí autonomic neurons|Ventral, lateral, dorsal columns|
|Ventral horn ‚Üí somatic motor neurons|Most tracts decussate (cross)|

**Clinical Focus:**  
Spinal cord injury ‚Üí paralysis/sensory loss below lesion.

**Exam Focus:**  
Draw labelled cross-section: dorsal, ventral, lateral horns + central canal.

---

## **12. Spinal Nerves**

- 31 pairs:
    
    - Cervical (8), Thoracic (12), Lumbar (5), Sacral (5), Coccygeal (1).
        
- Each forms by joining **dorsal (sensory)** and **ventral (motor)** roots.
    
- Mixed nerve branches into:
    
    - **Dorsal ramus** ‚Äì to back muscles/skin.
        
    - **Ventral ramus** ‚Äì to limbs and trunk.
        

**Clinical Focus:**  
Damage to a spinal root ‚Üí **radiculopathy** (dermatomal sensory loss).

---

## **13. Meninges**

Three protective layers covering CNS:

1. **Dura mater** ‚Äì tough outer layer.
    
    - Epidural space (between dura & bone).
        
    - Subdural space (potential).
        
2. **Arachnoid mater** ‚Äì web-like middle layer.
    
    - Subarachnoid space ‚Üí **contains CSF**.
        
3. **Pia mater** ‚Äì delicate inner layer, adheres to CNS surface, vascular.
    

**Clinical Focus:**

- **Meningitis:** inflammation of meninges.
    
- **Subdural / epidural haematoma:** bleeding between layers.
    

---

## **14. Ventricular System & Cerebrospinal Fluid (CSF)**

|Location|Ventricular Cavity|
|---|---|
|Cerebral hemispheres|Lateral ventricles|
|Diencephalon|Third ventricle|
|Between pons/medulla & cerebellum|Fourth ventricle|
|Spinal cord|Central canal|

**CSF production & flow:**

- Produced by **choroid plexus** in ventricles.
    
- Circulates ‚Üí ventricles ‚Üí subarachnoid space ‚Üí cisterns.
    
- Reabsorbed by **arachnoid granulations** into **dural venous sinuses**.
    

**Clinical Focus:**

- **Hydrocephalus:** CSF accumulation ‚Üí ‚Üë intracranial pressure.
    

**Exam Focus:**  
Trace **CSF pathway** and ventricles.

---

## **15. Sympathetic Nervous System (Thoracolumbar)**

- Origin: **T1‚ÄìL2 spinal segments**.
    
- **Preganglionic neurons:** in lateral horn.
    
- **Ganglia:** near vertebral column (sympathetic chain).
    
- **2-neuron chain:** preganglionic ‚Üí ganglion ‚Üí postganglionic neuron.
    

**Effects (‚ÄúFight or Flight‚Äù):**

- Pupil dilation
    
- ‚Üë Heart rate and BP
    
- Bronchodilation
    
- ‚Üë Glucose
    
- ‚Üì Digestive activity
    
- ‚Üë Sweating
    

**Clinical Focus:**  
Loss of sympathetic outflow ‚Üí **Horner‚Äôs syndrome** (ptosis, miosis, anhidrosis).

---

## **16. Parasympathetic Nervous System (Craniosacral)**

- Origin: **Brainstem (CN III, VII, IX, X)** + **S2‚ÄìS4** spinal segments.
    
- **Ganglia:** near or within target organ.
    
- **2-neuron chain** like sympathetic but longer preganglionic fibre.
    

**Effects (‚ÄúRest and Digest‚Äù):**

- Pupil constriction
    
- ‚Üë Salivation, digestion, urination, defecation
    
- ‚Üì Heart rate, respiration
    

**Clinical Focus:**  
Overactivation ‚Üí bradycardia, hypotension.
