## **4. Fertilisation**

- **Union of haploid sperm + haploid oocyte â†’ diploid zygote.**
    
- Usually occurs in **ampulla** of uterine tube.
    

### **Phases**![[Pasted image 20251104142525.png]]

Spermatozoon penetrates the oocyte in 3 phases.

1. **Capacitation (~7 h)** â€“ sperm membrane modified in female tract â†’ can pass **corona radiata**.

3. **Membrane fusion** â€“ one sperm enters; oocyte completes **meiosis II**.

**Result:** diploid **zygote (2n = 46)**.

## **5. Cleavage: Zygote â†’ Morula**

- Rapid **mitotic divisions** without growth.
    
- Once the zygote has reached the two-cell stage, it undergoes a series of mitotic divisions, increasing the number of cells.
- These cells, which become smaller with each cleavage division, are known as blastomeres.
- **Blastomeres** (daughter cells) become progressively smaller.
- Once the cell reaches beyond 16, it is called **morula**
	
- Stages:
    
    - 2-cell (~30 h)
        
    - 4-cell (~40 h)
        
    - 8-cell (~72 h)
        
    - **Morula (16-32 cells)** by day 3â€“4, still within **zona pellucida**.

## **10. Gastrulation â†’ Trilaminar Disc (Day 16)**

- **Epiblast cells** migrate through **primitive streak**, invaginate, and displace hypoblast.

- Cells of the epiblast migrate through the primitive streak, detach from the epiblast, displace hypoblast and create a third layer **(invagination)**
â†’ Formation of **three germ layers**:

## **13. Ectopic Pregnancy**

- **Abnormal implantation** of blastocyst outside uterine cavity.
- In the ovary or anywhere along the path of migration into the uterine cavity
- Placenta can form on most tissues or structures
    
- Sites:
    
    - **Tubal (â‰ˆ95 %)** â€“ ampulla or isthmus.
        
    - **Ovarian, cervical, abdominal** â€“ rare.

- **Tubal rupture + haemorrhage** (2ndâ€“5th month) â†’ maternal mortality risk.
    
- Sometimes causes **tubal abortion** (spontaneous loss).
- Can cause maternal mortality if undetected after intraperitoneal rupture of the uterine tube and its vessels
