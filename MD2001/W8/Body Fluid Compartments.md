## **2. Blood ‚Äì The Link Between Compartments**

- **Total blood ‚âà 5 L (7 % body weight)**.
    
- Contains **plasma (ECF)** + **RBC cytoplasm (ICF)**.
    
- Acts as the **transport medium** maintaining homeostasis between ICF and ECF.
    

**Clinical Focus:**  
Loss or dilution of blood ‚Üí rapid ECF disturbances (e.g. haemorrhage, dehydration, shock).

## **5. Biological Membranes**

### **Functions**

- Define cell boundaries.
    
- Regulate exchange of substances.
    
- Maintain ionic gradients for electrical and osmotic stability.
    
- Provide surface for receptors, enzymes, and communication.
    

### **Structure**

- **Phospholipid bilayer** (~6‚Äì10 nm thick).
    
- **Amphipathic:**
    
    - **Hydrophilic head** (faces water).
        
    - **Hydrophobic tails** (face inward).
        
- **Dynamic fluid mosaic**: lipids + proteins move laterally.
    

| Component                 | % of Membrane | Function                         |
| ------------------------- | ------------- | -------------------------------- |
| **Protein**               | 55 %          | Transporters, receptors, enzymes |
| **Phospholipid**          | 25 %          | Structural bilayer               |
| **Cholesterol**           | 13 %          | Membrane fluidity & stability    |
| **Carbohydrate (glyco-)** | 3‚Äì4 %         | Cell recognition, receptor sites |
| **Other lipids**          | 3%            |                                  |

## **6. Membrane Lipids**

- **Phospholipids:** basic bilayer structure, selective barrier.
    
- **Sphingolipids:** abundant in neural tissue; stability + signal transmission.
    
- **Cholesterol:** modulates **fluidity** (more cholesterol ‚Üí less fluid ‚Üí stronger barrier).
    

**Clinical Focus:**  
Altered lipid composition (e.g. hypercholesterolaemia) affects membrane integrity and receptor function.

## **7. Membrane Proteins**

|Type|Location|Function|
|---|---|---|
|**Integral proteins**|Span membrane|Channels, carriers, receptors|
|**Peripheral proteins**|Loosely attached to surface|Enzymes, cell signalling|

**Examples:**

- **Channel proteins** ‚Üí allow specific ions through pores. Free movement of water and some other molecules and ions
    
- **Carrier proteins** ‚Üí bind & transport molecules.
    
- **Receptors** ‚Üí detect signals (e.g. hormones).

## **9. Electrical and Pressure Gradients**

### **Electrical Potential**

- Ions move toward **opposite charges**.
    
- Can drive movement **against concentration gradient**.
    
- Basis for **membrane potentials** (especially in neurons and muscle).

The apical polarity of an epithelial cell has a net negative.
The basal polarity is net positive.
Overall, the cell itself inside is negative, the membrane of the basal helps it with the electrical potential to cross the membrane.

### **Pressure Difference**

- ‚Üë pressure = ‚Üë molecular motion ‚Üí ‚Üë diffusion rate.
    
- Example: **capillary filtration** (higher hydrostatic pressure inside ‚Üí fluid moves out).

## **10. Permeability Coefficient**

- Rate (cm/s) describing solute movement through membrane.
    
- Depends on **molecule size, charge, lipid solubility**, and **membrane structure**.
    
- High permeability = rapid diffusion (O‚ÇÇ, CO‚ÇÇ); low = restricted (ions, large proteins).

## **11. Osmosis**

- Diffusion of **water** through selectively permeable membranes.
    
- Driven by **solute concentration differences** (osmotic gradient).
    
- **Water moves from low solute ‚Üí high solute concentration.** (solute = concentration)
    
- **Osmotic pressure:** hydrostatic pressure needed to stop osmosis; measured in mmHg.
    

**Clinical Focus:**  
Plasma osmolality ‚âà 285‚Äì295 mOsm/kg H‚ÇÇO.  
Abnormalities ‚Üí neurological symptoms (brain cell swelling/shrinking).

## **12. Osmotic Equilibrium and Tonicity**

- **Osmotic equilibrium** maintained between ICF & ECF via water shifts.
    
- **Tonicity** = biological effect of solution on cell volume (depends on impermeant solutes). On a cell, in terms of shrinkage or swelling caused by placing cell in the solution of impermeant osmotically active particles.
    
![[Pasted image 20251104095705.png]]
The concentration of the water is higher in chamber B because the concentration of the water in chamber A has been diluted by the solutes.

|Solution|Water Flow|Effect on Cell|
|---|---|---|
|**Isotonic**|None|Normal shape|
|**Hypertonic**|Out of cell|Shrinks (crenation)|
|**Hypotonic**|Into cell|Swells ‚Üí may burst|
![[Pasted image 20251104100124.png]]

**Exam Focus:**  
Differentiate **tonicity vs osmolality** and describe cell responses.

**Clinical Focus:**

- IV fluids:
    
    - **0.9 % NaCl (isotonic)** ‚Äì expands ECF without affecting ICF.
        
    - **5 % dextrose (hypotonic)** ‚Äì water enters cells.
        
    - **3 % NaCl (hypertonic)** ‚Äì draws water out of cells (used for cerebral oedema).

## **13. Integration: Maintaining ICF‚ÄìECF Balance**
