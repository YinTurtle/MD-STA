## **1. What Does Microbiology Do?**

- **Identify the infecting organism** (bacteria, viruses, fungi, parasites). - diagnostics advice
    
- **Susceptibility testing** - determine **antibiotic resistance** to guide treatment.
    
- **Infection control** - identify **outbreaks** or clustered organisms in the community.
    

**Clinical Relevance:**  
Microbiology provides **critical information** about infection types and resistance patterns, enabling **targeted therapies** and **control of hospital-acquired infections**.

---

## **2. Laboratory Results and Their Role**

- **Diagnostic backup:** Laboratory results support clinical decisions.
    
- **Alert for suspected infections** not initially identified (e.g., **sepsis** confirmed via positive blood cultures).
    
- **Surveillance for outbreaks**: monitor circulating strains and resistance patterns.
    
- **Provide intelligence:** Guide **empirical antibiotic use** and **infection control** efforts.
    

**Clinical Focus:**

- **Sepsis management** often requires rapid microbiology results to guide treatment decisions.
    
- **Antibiotic stewardship** relies on understanding pathogen resistance profiles.

## **4. Roles of the Clinical Microbiologist**

1. **Provide diagnostic tests** for pathogens.
    
2. **Consultation service** for complex infections.
    
3. **Therapeutic advice** for serious infections (e.g., **empiric treatment** for septic shock).
    
4. **Infection control** within hospitals: **screening**, **outbreak management**.
    

**Clinical Relevance:**

- Effective microbiological consultations ensure **timely diagnosis** and **correct treatment** of infections, **reducing mortality** from conditions like **sepsis**.

## **5. How to Make a Microbiological Diagnosis**

### **Methods:**

1. **Direct examination:**
    
    - **Smears** (rapid, low sensitivity, requires expertise). - serology - test for a blood serum
        
    - **Cultures:** Grow organisms on agar, test for susceptibility.
        
    - **Molecular techniques:** PCR, **Next-Gen Sequencing** for fast pathogen identification.
        

**Clinical Focus:**

- PCR for rapid identification of **viral respiratory infections** (e.g., **Mycoplasma pneumoniae**, **Chlamydia psittaci**).
    
- **MALDI-TOF** mass spectrometry for bacterial identification.

- **Advantages:** Rapid, simple, inexpensive.
    
- **Disadvantages:** Low **sensitivity** and **specificity**, requires expert interpretation.

## **7. Culture Diagnosis**

- **Gold standard**: Most **sensitive** for identifying organisms.
    
- Allows **susceptibility testing** (determine best antibiotics).
    
- Disadvantage: **Slow growth**, cultures may be **rendered negative** if pre-treated with antibiotics.
    

**Clinical Focus:**

- Negative cultures can occur if the patient has been **pre-treated** with antibiotics before sample collection.
    

## **MALDI-TOF**
- positive step

- rapid identification of bacteria
- does not provide susceptibilities
- delayed by slow growth
- of no value if antibiotics render cultures negative

## **8. Serological Diagnosis**

- Detects antibodies or antigens to a pathogen.
    
- **IgM** and **IgA** for **acute infection**; **IgG** for **past exposure**.
    
- Detects **antigen** (e.g., **HIV**, **Hepatitis**).
    

**Clinical Focus:**

- **Serological tests** are important for diagnosing **viral infections** (e.g., **HIV, CMV**).
    
- **Titres** help track infection progress (e.g., **rising titres** for **rubella**).

### **Examples of serological techniques**
- Agglutination
- Precipitation
- Complement fixation
- Virus neutralization
- Enzyme linked immunosorbent assay (ELISA)
- Radioimmunoassay (RIA)
- Immunofluorescence

## **10. Sensitivity & Specificity**

- **Sensitivity**: Ability of a test to **identify true positives** (correctly detect infection).
    
    - **Formula**: Sensitivity = (True Positives) / (True Positives + False Negatives).
        
- **Specificity**: Ability of a test to **identify true negatives** (correctly rule out infection).
    
    - **Formula**: Specificity = (True Negatives) / (True Negatives + False Positives).

## **13. Molecular Techniques in Pneumonia Diagnosis**

- **Real-time PCR** for rapid **detection of bacterial pathogens** like **S. pneumoniae** and **H. influenzae**.
    
- Faster results than traditional **culture methods**, crucial for **targeted antibiotic therapy**.
    

**Clinical Focus:**

- **Molecular tests** allow for **rapid identification** of pathogens in **community-acquired pneumonia** (CAP), reducing unnecessary antibiotic use.
