## **1. Resting Membrane Potential (RMP)**

Typical ionic composition:

|Ion|Inside (mM)|Outside (mM)|
|---|---|---|
|**Na‚Å∫**|10|140|
|**K‚Å∫**|140|5|

At rest:

- **K‚Å∫** moves **out** (down concentration gradient).
    
- Membrane more **permeable to K‚Å∫** than Na‚Å∫ (‚âà 50√ó).
    
- Loss of K‚Å∫ leaves inside **negative** (relative to outside).  
    ‚Üí Generates the **resting potential** (‚âà ‚Äì70 mV).
    

**Key Concept:**  
The RMP is dominated by **K‚Å∫ diffusion potential**, but slightly depolarised from the **K‚Å∫ equilibrium potential (E‚Çñ ‚âà ‚Äì90 mV)** due to small Na‚Å∫ permeability.

## **2. Ion Equilibrium Potentials**

- Determined by **Nernst equation**.
    
- Represent the potential where **no net ionic movement** occurs.
    

|Ion|Equilibrium Potential (approx.)|
|---|---|
|**K‚Å∫ (E‚Çñ)**|‚Äì90 mV|
|**Na‚Å∫ (E‚Çô‚Çê)**|+60 mV|

- If the cell were **only permeable to K‚Å∫**, Vm = E‚Çñ (‚âà ‚Äì90 mV).
    
- If **only permeable to Na‚Å∫**, Vm = E‚Çô‚Çê (+60 mV).
    
- In reality, permeability (P) differs for each ion:
    
    - At rest **PNa : PK = 1 : 50**.

## **3. Origin of Resting Membrane Potential**

- **K‚Å∫ efflux** creates a **negative intracellular charge**.
    
- This charge attracts K‚Å∫ back inward (electrical gradient).
    
- At equilibrium, **chemical and electrical forces balance**.

## **5. Key Terms**

|Term|Definition|
|---|---|
|**Depolarisation**|Membrane potential becomes **less negative** (toward 0).|
|**Repolarisation**|Return to **resting potential** after depolarisation.|
|**Hyperpolarisation**|Becomes **more negative** than resting.|
|**Threshold**|Minimum depolarisation needed to trigger AP (‚âà ‚Äì55 mV).|

## **7. Characteristics of an Action Potential**

- Occurs in **excitable cells** (neurons, muscle).
    
- **Rapid, transient depolarisation** followed by repolarisation.
    
- **All-or-none**: once threshold reached, full AP occurs (no partial).
    
- **Depolarising phase:** inward Na‚Å∫ current.
    
- **Repolarising phase:** outward K‚Å∫ current.
    
- **Overshoot:** potential becomes positive (~ +30 mV).
    
- **After-hyperpolarisation:** brief period below resting level.

|---|---|---|---|

|**Depolarisation (upstroke)**|Na‚Å∫ influx|‚Üí +30 mV|PNa ‚Üë (~600√ó)|
|**Repolarisation**|K‚Å∫ efflux|Back to ‚Äì70 mV|PK ‚Üë (~10√ó), PNa ‚Üì|

## **9. Membrane Permeability Changes**

- At rest: **PNa : PK = 1 : 50**.
    
- During upstroke: **PNa ‚Üë 600-fold**.
    
- During repolarisation: **PK ‚Üë 10-fold**.  
    ‚Üí These permeability shifts generate the AP shape.

## **10. Voltage-Gated Ion Channels**

### **A. Voltage-Gated Na‚Å∫ Channels**

- **Closed** at rest.
    
- **Open rapidly** on depolarisation ‚Üí Na‚Å∫ influx ‚Üí further depolarisation.
    
- **Inactivate** after a few milliseconds (time-dependent).
    
- Return to **closed state** during repolarisation.
    

### **B. Voltage-Gated K‚Å∫ Channels**

- **Closed** at rest.
    
- **Open slowly** during depolarisation ‚Üí K‚Å∫ efflux ‚Üí repolarisation.
    
- **Stay open briefly** after repolarisation ‚Üí hyperpolarisation.
    

---

## **11. Positive Feedback in Depolarisation**

1. Small depolarisation ‚Üí few Na‚Å∫ channels open.
    
2. Na‚Å∫ influx depolarises membrane further.
    
3. More channels open ‚Üí **regenerative Na‚Å∫ entry**.
    
4. Results in the **upstroke** of the AP.
    

**Self-limiting mechanism:** Na‚Å∫ channels inactivate and K‚Å∫ channels open ‚Üí repolarisation.

---

## **12. Relationship Between Voltage and Permeability**

- **Depolarisation** increases Na‚Å∫ channel opening probability.
    
- As Na‚Å∫ channels inactivate, **K‚Å∫ channels** dominate ‚Üí repolarisation.
    
- The sequential change in **PNa** and **PK** drives voltage changes across time.
    

---

## **13. Threshold Potential**

- **Critical membrane voltage (~ ‚Äì55 mV)** required to trigger AP.
    
- Below threshold ‚Üí graded potential only (fails to trigger).
    
- Above threshold ‚Üí all-or-none AP generated.
    

**Clinical Focus:**

- **Local anaesthetics (e.g., lidocaine)** block **voltage-gated Na‚Å∫ channels**, preventing depolarisation and blocking nerve conduction.

## **14. Phases of the Action Potential**

|Phase|Main Ion Movement|Vm (mV)|Channels Active|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Resting**|K‚Å∫ out (leak)|‚Äì70|K‚Å∫ leak only|
|**Depolarisation**|Na‚Å∫ in|+30|Na‚Å∫ open|
|**Repolarisation**|K‚Å∫ out|‚Äì70|K‚Å∫ open, Na‚Å∫ inactivated|
|**Hyperpolarisation**|Excess K‚Å∫ outflow|‚Äì80|K‚Å∫ closing slowly|
|**Return to Rest**|Na‚Å∫/K‚Å∫ ATPase restores|‚Äì70|Pump active|

## **15. The All-or-None Principle**

- Once threshold reached, AP amplitude and shape **do not vary** with stimulus strength.
    
- Below threshold ‚Üí **no AP**.
    
- Above threshold ‚Üí **identical AP**.
    

---

## **16. Refractory Periods**

|Type|Description|Function|
|---|---|---|
|**Absolute refractory period**|Na‚Å∫ channels inactivated ‚Üí no new AP possible|Prevents backfiring, sets AP direction|
|**Relative refractory period**|Some Na‚Å∫ channels recovered; stronger stimulus required|Allows frequency control|

## **19. Clinical Relevance**

- **Channelopathies:** genetic defects in ion channels ‚Üí altered excitability.
    
    - _Long QT syndrome_ (defective K‚Å∫ channels) ‚Üí arrhythmias.
        
    - _Episodic paralysis_ (Na‚Å∫ channel mutations).
        
- **Local anaesthetics**: block Na‚Å∫ channels ‚Üí prevent depolarisation.
    
- **Neurotoxins:**
    
    - _Tetrodotoxin (pufferfish)_ ‚Üí blocks Na‚Å∫ channels.
        
    - _TEA (tetraethylammonium)_ ‚Üí blocks K‚Å∫ channels.
