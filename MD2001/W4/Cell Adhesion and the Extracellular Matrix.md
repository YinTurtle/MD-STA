## üß© **1. Connective Tissue Overview**

Connective tissue is like the ‚Äú**support network**‚Äù of the body ‚Äî it fills the spaces between organs, supports epithelia, and connects and cushions other tissues.

### üß± Composition

Connective tissue = **Cells + Extracellular Matrix (ECM)**

### üß¨ Extracellular Matrix (ECM)

- The **non-cellular scaffold** that surrounds cells and gives structural + biochemical support.
    
- Contains:
    
    - **Protein fibres:**
        
        - **Collagen** ‚Üí strength
            
        - **Elastin** ‚Üí stretch & recoil
            
        - **Reticular fibres** ‚Üí delicate support (lymphoid tissue)
            
    - **Ground substance:**
        
        - A gel made of **proteoglycans**, **glycoproteins**, and **GAGs** (glycosaminoglycans).
            
        - Provides cushioning and allows diffusion of nutrients.
            

### üß´ Types of Connective Tissue

1. **Loose connective tissue** ‚Üí more cells, less fibre; under epithelia, around organs.
    
2. **Dense connective tissue** ‚Üí more fibres, fewer cells; tendons, ligaments, dermis.

## üß¨ **2. The Extracellular Matrix in Detail**

The ECM doesn‚Äôt just hold cells in place ‚Äî it _talks_ to them.  
It influences **cell growth, migration, gene expression, and repair**.

### üß± Structural & Functional Roles

- Provides a **framework** that supports cells.
    
- **Transmits signals** to cells through cell-surface receptors (e.g., integrins).
    
- Varies between tissues:
    
    - **Tendons:** mostly fibrous (collagen) ‚Üí tensile strength.
        
    - **Cartilage:** rich in polysaccharides ‚Üí compressibility.
        
    - **Basement membrane (BM):** specialised ECM supporting epithelia.

## üß´ **3. Main Cells of Connective Tissue**

### üßµ **Fibroblasts**

- The **chief builders** of the ECM.
    
- Secrete **collagen**, **elastin**, and **ground substance**.
    
- Maintain structural framework of tissues.
    

> Think of fibroblasts as the ‚Äúconstruction workers‚Äù maintaining tissue integrity.

### ‚öôÔ∏è **Myofibroblasts**

- Intermediate between **fibroblasts** and **smooth muscle cells**.
    
- Contain **actin**, **myosin**, and **desmin** ‚Üí allow contraction.
    
- Appear during **wound healing** and **fibrosis**.
    
    - They **proliferate**, **secrete collagen**, and **contract** the wound to close it.
        
- Overactivation ‚Üí **fibrotic scarring** (e.g., cirrhosis).
    

### üç© **Adipocytes (Fat Cells)**

- Store triglycerides ‚Üí **energy reserve**.
    
- Provide **insulation** and **padding** around organs.
    
- Secrete **leptin**, a hormone that tells the hypothalamus about fat stores.
    
    - In obesity, leptin resistance develops ‚Üí continued eating despite high levels.
        

### üß® **Mast Cells**

- Contain **granules with histamine and heparin**.
    
- Release these mediators in **inflammatory and allergic reactions**.
    
- Found near blood vessels and mucosal surfaces.
    

### üß´ **Plasma Cells**

- Derived from **B lymphocytes**.
    
- Produce and secrete **antibodies (immunoglobulins)**.
    
- Short lifespan (~10‚Äì30 days), limited mobility.
    

### üßπ **Macrophages**

- Derived from **monocytes** (blood).
    
- ‚ÄúProfessional phagocytes‚Äù: engulf pathogens and dead cells.
    
- Contain many **lysosomes**.
    
- Present antigens on **MHC Class II** molecules to activate T-cells.

## üß± **4. ECM Components**

### **A. Fibrous Proteins**

1. **Collagen** ‚Äì strength
    
2. **Elastin** ‚Äì elasticity
    
3. **Fibronectin** ‚Äì adhesive link
    
4. **Laminin** ‚Äì adhesion in basement membranes
    

### **B. Polysaccharides (GAGs) and Proteoglycans**

- Long chains of repeating disaccharides:
    
    - **N-acetylglucosamine / N-acetylgalactosamine**
        
    - **Glucuronic / iduronic acid**
        
- Negatively charged ‚Üí attract cations + water ‚Üí **hydrated gel** for support and cushioning.
    
- **Sulfated GAGs:** dermatan, chondroitin, keratan, heparan.
    
- **Hyaluronan:** unsulfated, free polysaccharide (lubrication, cell migration).
    
- GAGs + protein core = **proteoglycan** (~95% sugar by weight).

## üß¨ **5. Collagen**

### üî¨ Structure

- Triple helix made of 3 Œ±-chains.
    
- Repeating amino acid pattern: **Gly‚ÄìX‚ÄìY**
    
    - X = proline, Y = hydroxyproline.
        
- Stabilised by hydrogen bonds.
    
- Hydroxylation of proline and lysine requires **vitamin C**.
    

### üß™ Biosynthesis Summary

1. Synthesised as **procollagen Œ±-chains** in RER.
    
2. Hydroxylation (vit C dependent).
    
3. Glycosylation ‚Üí oligosaccharides added.
    
4. Formation of triple helix (assisted by **Hsp47** chaperone).
    
5. Packaged in Golgi and secreted.
    
6. Cleavage of terminal peptides ‚Üí **collagen fibril** forms.
    
7. Fibrils cross-link ‚Üí **collagen fibre**.
    

### üß© Collagen Types

|Type|Location|Function|
|---|---|---|
|I|Bone, skin, tendon|Strength, most common|
|II|Cartilage|Resistance to pressure|
|III|Reticular fibres|Support in soft tissue|
|IV|Basal lamina|Flexible network|

**Clinical:**

- Vitamin C deficiency ‚Üí poor hydroxylation ‚Üí weak collagen ‚Üí **scurvy**.

## ü™∂ **6. Elastin**

- Main component of **elastic fibres**.
    
- Crosslinked via **lysine side chains**.
    
- Provides **stretch and recoil** ‚Üí ‚Äúbiological rubber.‚Äù
    
- Found in **lungs, arteries, skin**.
    
- Loss = stiffness (e.g., ageing, emphysema).
    

---

## üíß **7. ECM Polysaccharides (GAGs & Proteoglycans)**

### **GAGs Structure**

Repeating disaccharides (e.g., GlcNAc + glucuronic acid).  
Highly hydrophilic ‚Üí trap water ‚Üí form **gel-like matrix**.  
Allows diffusion of nutrients and metabolites.

### **Proteoglycans**

- GAGs attached to a core protein.
    
- Form hydrated networks that cushion and resist compression.
    
- Also ‚Äúglue‚Äù ECM components together and bind cell surface receptors.

## üß≤ **8. Adhesion Proteins**

They **link ECM components** and **connect ECM to cells**.

|Adhesion Protein|Key Points|
|---|---|
|**Fibronectin**|Dimeric glycoprotein in connective tissue; binds collagen, proteoglycans, and integrins.|
|**Laminin**|Found in basal lamina; cross-shaped trimer (Œ±, Œ≤, Œ≥). Links cells to type IV collagen.|
|**Integrins**|Transmembrane receptors linking ECM ‚Üí actin cytoskeleton via adaptor proteins. Essential for signalling, movement, and wound healing.|

## üß¨ **9. Linking ECM to Cytoskeleton**

- **Collagen/proteoglycans** bind **fibronectin**, which binds **integrins** on the cell surface.
    
- Inside the cell, integrins connect to **actin filaments** via adaptor proteins.

> This is how mechanical signals (e.g. stretch) and biochemical cues are transmitted between ECM and cell.

## ü©π **10. Wound Healing and Fibrosis**

- **Fibroblasts** ‚Üí secrete new collagen.
    
- **Myofibroblasts** ‚Üí contract to close wound.
    
- **Macrophages** ‚Üí clean debris and secrete growth factors.
    
- Chronic injury ‚Üí **excess collagen** ‚Üí fibrosis (scar).

## ‚öôÔ∏è **11. Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy (DMD)**

- **X-linked recessive** mutation in **dystrophin** gene.
    
- Dystrophin anchors the **muscle cytoskeleton to the ECM** via a protein complex.
    
- Without it ‚Üí muscle fibre tears during contraction ‚Üí degeneration.
    
- Progressive muscle weakness ‚Üí pseudohypertrophy of calves, Gower‚Äôs sign.
    
- **Experimental therapy:**
    
    - **Ataluren (NICE-approved)** allows ribosomes to ‚Äúread through‚Äù premature stop codons in dystrophin mRNA.

## üß† **12. ECM and Cancer**

### üß¨ Stages of Cancer Progression

1. **Carcinoma in situ**
    
    - Cells abnormal but still above the **basement membrane**.
        
2. **Epithelial-to-Mesenchymal Transition (EMT)**
    
    - Loss of **E-cadherin** ‚Üí cells become motile (‚Äúmesenchymal‚Äù).
        
3. **Microinvasion**
    
    - Formation of **invadipodia** (actin-based protrusions).
        
    - Secretion of **metalloproteinases (MMPs)** ‚Üí degrade basement membrane.
        
4. **Metastasis**
    
    - Cells enter blood/lymph vessels.
        
    - Express **integrins** to attach to new ECM.
        
    - Secrete **VEGF** ‚Üí angiogenesis (new blood vessels).
        

**Clinical connection:**  
Targeting VEGF (e.g., with **bevacizumab**) helps block tumour vascularisation.
