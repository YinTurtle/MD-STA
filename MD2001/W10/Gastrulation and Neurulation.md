# **Learning Outcomes**

By end of lecture, you must be able to:

- **Describe sequence & significance of gastrulation**
    
- **Explain neural tube formation** (neurulation)
    
- **Describe origin & derivatives of neural crest cells**
    
- **Know derivatives of ectoderm, mesoderm, endoderm**  
    

**Exam prediction:** MCQs will almost certainly ask

- _“Which germ layer gives rise to X?”_
    
- _“Failure of posterior neuropore closure leads to?”_
    

---

# **Slide 3 – The First Week**

  
Sequence:

1. **Ovulation**
    
2. **Fertilisation** in **ampulla** of uterine tube
    
3. Migration to uterus
    
4. **Cleavage → morula → blastocyst**
    
5. Implantation completes by **day 9**
    

**Important:**

- Blastocyst = **inner cell mass (embryoblast)** + **trophoblast**
    
- At this stage embryo is still **bilaminar**.
    

---

# **Slide 4 – End of Week 2**

Key structures:

- **Chorionic sac** surrounding embryo
    
- **Epiblast (dorsal)** + **Hypoblast (ventral)**
    
- **Amniotic sac** (dorsal)
    
- **Yolk sac** (ventral)
    
- **Connecting stalk** (future umbilical cord)
    

**Day 14:**

- **Primitive streak** appears in caudal epiblast
    
- This marks the **start of gastrulation**  
    → Epiblast cells begin **migration, invagination, replacement**.
    

---

# **Slide 5 – Gastrulation**

  
Quote by Wolpert emphasises:

> “Gastrulation is the most important time in your life.”

**Why?**

- This is when **bilaminar disc → trilaminar disc**:
    
    - **Ectoderm**
        
    - **Mesoderm**
        
    - **Endoderm**
        
- Establishes the **body axes**.
    

---

# **Slide 6 – Primitive Streak, Groove, Pit, Node**

Features:

- **Primitive streak** (caudal midline)
    
- **Primitive groove**
    
- **Primitive pit**
    
- **Primitive node** = **organising centre**
    

**Function of node:**

- Coordinates **cell migration**, **notochord formation**, **dorsal-ventral patterning**.
    

---

# **Slide 7 – Cell Migration 1 (Endoderm Formation)**

Process:

1. Epiblast cells **invaginate** through primitive streak
    
2. They **replace hypoblast**
    
3. These migrating cells become **definitive endoderm**
    

**Important distinctions:**

- **Hypoblast does NOT become endoderm**; it is replaced.
    
- Endoderm becomes **gut tube lining, respiratory lining**.
    

---

# **Slide 8 – Cell Migration 2 (Mesoderm Formation)**

Second wave:

- Epiblast cells enter streak, fill space **between epiblast and newly formed endoderm** → **intraembryonic mesoderm**.
    

Except at two membrane locations:

- **Oropharyngeal membrane** (future mouth)
    
- **Cloacal membrane** (future anus)
    

Epiblast remaining at surface becomes **ectoderm**.

# **Slide 9 – (Blank/Transition Slide)**

Used to move toward notochord formation.

# **Slide 10 – Notochord**

Sequence:

- From **primitive pit**, a tube-like extension grows cranially through mesoderm → **notochordal process**
    
- Transforms
    
    - Tube → Plate → Solid rod (**notochord**) by days **17–20**
        

Functions:

- **Defines midline**
    
- Establishes **dorsal–ventral axis**
    
- Induces **neurulation** (neural plate → neural tube)
    
- Later forms **nucleus pulposus** in intervertebral discs.
    

---

# **Slide 11 – Dorsal/Ventral Axis Patterning**

Signals:

- **BMPs** promote **ventral** (skin) fate
    
- **Node + notochord produce BMP inhibitors**:
    
    - **Chordin**
        
    - **Noggin**
        
    - **Follistatin**
        

This shifts dorsal fate to **neural tissue**, not skin.

**Important experiment:**

- Spemann & Mangold (1924) — node transplantation → **twin dorsal axis**.  
    → The node = **primary organiser**.
    

---

# **Slide 12 – Neurulation (Induction by the Notochord)**

Steps:

1. Notochord signals ectoderm above to thicken → **neural plate**
    
2. Neural plate → **neural groove** (~day 19)
    
3. Edges form **neural folds**
    

---

# **Slide 13 – Neural Fold & Groove Formation**

Day **20–21**:

- Lateral edges elevate
    
- Groove deepens
    
- Neural folds are precursors of **neural crest cells**
    

---

# **Slide 14 – Neural Tube Formation (Primary Neurulation)**

By day **22**:

- Folds fuse to create **neural tube**
    
- Fusion begins in **cervical region** → proceeds cranially and caudally
    

Morphology driven by **apical constriction**, actin-myosin contraction.

---

# **Slide 15 – Closure of Neural Tube**

Mechanism:

- “Zips up” both directions
    

Key events:

- **Anterior neuropore closes ~day 25**
    
- **Posterior neuropore closes ~day 28**
    

**Secondary neurulation:**

- Caudal end forms **medullary cord** → cavitates → joins neural tube.
    

Rostral end forms **3 primary brain vesicles**.

---

# **Slide 16 – Neural Tube Defects**

Failure of **neuropore closure** → defects:

- **Anterior neuropore:**
    
    - Anencephaly / craniorachischisis
        
- **Posterior neuropore:**
    
    - **Spina bifida** (occulta, meningocele, myelomeningocele)
        

---

# **Slide 17 – Neural Crest Cells (NCCs)**

Neural crest arises from:

- **Lateral edges of neural folds**
    

Process:

- Undergo **epithelial → mesenchymal transition (EMT)**
    
- Migrate extensively
    

Major derivatives (very high yield):

- **Sensory (DRG) ganglia**
    
- **Autonomic ganglia**
    
- **Schwann cells**
    
- **Adrenal medulla (chromaffin cells)**
    
- **Melanocytes**
    
- **Craniofacial cartilage/bone**
    
- **Odontoblasts**
    
- **C cells of thyroid**
    
- **Outflow tract smooth muscle**
    

---

# **Slide 18 – Neural Crest Cell Problems**

Images imply pigmentation defects → **melanocyte migration issues**.

---

# **Slide 19 – Neural Crest–Linked Syndromes**

### **Waardenburg Syndrome**

- Pax3 mutation
    
- Pigmentation defects, heterochromia, deafness, telecanthus
    
- Can include **Hirschsprung disease** (aganglionic colon)
    

### **Treacher Collins Syndrome**

- TCOF1 mutation (Treacle)
    
- Craniofacial malformations
    
- Underdeveloped zygoma, micrognathia, malformed ears
    
- Neural crest apoptosis → facial defects

# **Slide 20 – Mesoderm Formation Summary**

From epiblast migration:

- Replaces hypoblast → **endoderm**
    
- Middle layer → **mesoderm**
    
- Remaining epiblast → **ectoderm**
    

---

# **Slide 21 – Types of Mesoderm**

Three subdivisions:

- **Paraxial**
    
- **Intermediate**
    
- **Lateral plate mesoderm (LPM)**
    

---

# **Slide 22 – Mesoderm Cross-Section**

Shows paraxial → somites  
LPM splits into

- **Somatic (parietal)**
    
- **Splanchnic (visceral)**

# **Slide 23 – Somites**

Formation (day 20–30):

- 3–4 pairs/day
    
- Initially 42–44 → regress to **~37**
    

Somite organization:

- 4 occipital
    
- 8 cervical
    
- 12 thoracic
    
- 5 lumbar
    
- 5 sacral
    
- 3 coccygeal
    

---

# **Slide 24 – Connection to Spinal Segments**

Somite segmentation matches **spinal nerve distribution**.

---

# **Slide 25–26 – Somite Formation Mechanism**

Regulated by:

- **FGF8 wavefront** moving caudally
    
- **Notch signalling “clock”** oscillating every **~90 minutes**
    

Outcome:

- Early in cycle → **anterior** somite portion
    
- Late in cycle → **posterior** portion
    

---

# **Slide 27 – HOX Genes**

Key concept:

- Somites acquire **regional identity** via **HOX gene expression**
    
- Embryo “counts” somites to position HOX boundaries
    
- Mutations → homeotic transformations (e.g., **Antennapedia**).
    

---

# **Slide 28–30 – Mesoderm Derivatives**

### **Paraxial Mesoderm**

- **Dermis**, **skeletal muscle**, **axial skeleton**
    

### **Intermediate Mesoderm**

- **Kidneys**, **gonads**, **ureters**, **reproductive ducts**
    

### **Lateral Plate Mesoderm**

- **Heart**, **blood vessels**, **blood**
    
- **Limb bones**, **limb cartilage**
    
- **Body wall**, **spleen**
    
- **Visceral serosa**, **parietal serosa**
    

---

# **Slide 31 – Midline Mesoderm**

- **Prechordal plate** = future mouth area
    
- **Nucleus pulposus** of intervertebral discs comes from notochord
    

---

# **Slide 33–35 – Endoderm**

### Folding:

- **Craniocaudal and lateral folding** → pinches endoderm → **primitive gut tube**
    

### Derivatives:

- **GI tract lining**
    
- **Respiratory tract lining**
    
- **Distal urinary tract**
    
- **Endocrine glands** (thyroid, parathyroid)
    
- Connections: vitelline duct → potential remnant
    
    - **Meckel's diverticulum** (rule of 2’s)
        

---

# **Slide 36 – Ectoderm Derivatives**

Includes:

- **Epidermis & glands**
    
- **Hair, nails, enamel**
    
- **Cornea, lens**
    
- **Olfactory epithelium**
    
- **Oral and anal epithelium**
    
- **Mammary & salivary glands**
    
- **Adenohypophysis**
    
- **Terminal male urethra, labia majora/minora**
    

(Plus neural tube & crest derivatives)

# **High-Yield Summary

### **Gastrulation:**

- Primitive streak → epiblast migrates → **endoderm, mesoderm, ectoderm**
    

### **Notochord:**

- Defines axis, induces neural plate, becomes **nucleus pulposus**
    

### **Neurulation:**

- Neural plate → fold → tube
    
- Ant. neuropore (day 25), Post. neuropore (day 28)
    

### **Neural Crest Cells:**

- DRG, autonomic ganglia, Schwann cells, adrenal medulla, melanocytes, facial bones
    

### **Mesoderm:**

- Paraxial → somites (muscle, dermis, axial skeleton)
    
- Intermediate → kidneys, gonads
    
- Lateral plate → heart, limbs, vessels
    

### **Endoderm:**

- Gut tube, respiratory lining, thyroid, bladder
    

### **Ectoderm:**

- Skin, enamel, lens, pituitary, neural tissues
