# üîä **2. SOUND & ULTRASOUND PHYSICS (Slides 3‚Äì10)**

### What is sound?

- Sound = **mechanical longitudinal wave**
    
- Alternating **compression** (‚Üëpressure) & **rarefaction** (‚Üìpressure)
    
- Requires **medium** to travel  
    

### Amplitude

- Height of waveform
    
- In US: larger amplitude ‚Üí **brighter echo** on screen  
    

### Frequency

- Cycles per second, measured in **Hertz**
    
- Audible range: **20 Hz ‚Äì 20 kHz**
    
- Ultrasound: **> 20 kHz**
    
- Diagnostic ultrasound: **1‚Äì20 MHz**  
    

### HIGH VS LOW FREQUENCY

|Frequency|Pros|Cons|
|---|---|---|
|**High (10‚Äì15 MHz)**|Great resolution|Poor depth|
|**Low (1‚Äì5 MHz)**|Deep penetration|Poor resolution|

Exam tip: **High = detail, low = depth**.

# üì° **4. DIAGNOSTIC ULTRASOUND ‚Äì WHAT HAPPENS IN THE BODY? (Slides 16‚Äì17)**

- Machine sends **short sound pulses**
    
- Sound undergoes:
    
    - **Reflection** ‚Üí main imaging mechanism
        
    - **Refraction**
        
    - **Scattering**
        
    - **Absorption**  
        ‚Üí All these cause **attenuation**, limiting depth  
        

**Clinical implication:** Deep obese patients often require **low-frequency curvilinear probe**.

# ‚ö° **6. PIEZOELECTRIC EFFECT (Slides 22‚Äì23)**

### Piezoelectric effect

- **Stress ‚Üí electrical charge**
    

### Reverse piezoelectric effect

- **Electrical voltage ‚Üí mechanical vibration** (sound waves)
    

**Piezoelectric crystals:**

- Generate ultrasound pulses
    
- Receive returning echoes
    
- Convert echo ‚Üí electrical signal ‚Üí image pixel  
    

**High amplitude echo ‚Üí bright pixel**.

# üîå **7. TRANSDUCERS / PROBES (Slides 24‚Äì29)**

## **7A. LINEAR PROBE**

- **High frequency (6‚Äì15 MHz)**
    
- **Superficial structures**
    
- Flat footprint ‚Üí no distortion  
    **Uses:** vascular access, tendons, nerves, thyroid  
    

---

## **7B. CURVILINEAR PROBE**

- **Low frequency (1‚Äì5 MHz)**
    
- **Deep structures**
    
- Wide field view but slightly distorted  
    **Uses:** abdomen, FAST scan  
    

---

## **7C. PHASED-ARRAY PROBE**

- **Small footprint**
    
- Produces ‚Äú**sector image**‚Äù
    
- Good for **between ribs**  
    **Uses:** cardiac (TTE)  
    

---

## **7D. INTRALUMINAL PROBES**

- High frequency
    
- Inserted into lumen (vagina, rectum, oesophagus)  
    **Uses:** EV scan, cardiac TEE

# üéõ **8. IMAGE MODES (Slides 30‚Äì39)**

## **B-MODE (‚ÄúBrightness mode‚Äù)**

- Produces 2D grayscale image
    
- Brightness = amplitude
    
- Position = depth  
    

**Echogenicity terms:**

- **Isoechoic**: same brightness
    
- **Hyperechoic**: whiter
    
- **Hypoechoic**: darker
    
- **Anechoic**: black (fluid)
    

---

## **DOPPLER MODE**

- Based on **Doppler effect** (frequency shift from moving blood)
    
- Color Doppler uses **BART**:
    
    - **Blue Away**
        
    - **Red Toward**  
        

**Uses:** DVT, blood flow, vascular stenosis, cardiac valves.

---

## **M-MODE**

- Motion along a single line
    
- Excellent temporal resolution  
    **Uses:** cardiac valve motion, fetal heart rate  
    

---

## **3D / 4D IMAGING**

- Stacks B-mode slices ‚Üí 3D
    
- Common in **obstetric morphology scans**

# üéö **9. BASIC MACHINE CONTROLS (Slides 40‚Äì48)**

## **Frequency setting: Res / Gen / Pen**

- **Res** ‚Üí high freq ‚Üí superficial
    
- **Pen** ‚Üí low freq ‚Üí deep  
    

---

## **Gain**

- Amplifies returning echoes
    
- Too much = snowstorm artefact
    
- NOT screen brightness  
    

---

## **Depth & Scale**

- Adjust so structure of interest fills **‚Öî of screen**  
    

---

## **Presets**

- Automatically adjust power, depth, gain for specific anatomy  
    

---

## **Probe orientation**

- **Marker** corresponds to left side of screen
    
- Longitudinal: marker = cephalic
    
- Transverse: marker = patient‚Äôs right  
    

---

## **Probe manoeuvres (PART)**

- **P Pressure**
    
- **A Alignment (sliding)**
    
- **R Rotation**
    
- **T Tilt**  
    

Tilt crucial for avoiding anisotropy.

# ‚ö†Ô∏è **10. ULTRASOUND ARTEFACTS (Slides 49‚Äì56)**

## **Acoustic Shadowing**

- Bone, stones, metal ‚Üí no transmission ‚Üí dark shadow  
    

## **Acoustic Enhancement**

- Fluid passes sound easily ‚Üí bright area behind
    
- Seen behind: bladder, gallbladder  
    

## **Anisotropy**

- Seen in tendons ‚Üí brightness depends on angle
    
- Can **mimic tendon tear**

# üß¨ **11. RISKS & SAFETY (Ultrasound 2, Slides 1‚Äì9)**

Ultrasound is **non-ionising** but not 0-risk:

- Can cause **tissue heating**
    
- **Cavitation** (gas bubble formation)  
    

## **Thermal Index (TI)**

- Predicts **tissue heating** potential.
    

## **Mechanical Index (MI)**

- Predicts **cavitation** risk.  
    

## **ALARA principle**

‚ÄúAs Low As Reasonably Achievable‚Äù

- Scan only when indicated
    
- Lowest power
    
- Shortest time
    
- Watch TI, MI, exposure duration  
    

### Obstetric scans

- Keep **TI < 1, MI < 1**
    
- Minimise Doppler in first trimester

# üè• **12. CLINICAL APPLICATIONS (Ultrasound 2)**

---

## **A. POCUS (Point-of-Care Ultrasound)**

- Bedside, goal-directed
    
- Increases diagnostic accuracy immediately  
    

**Examples:**

- Shock ‚Üí IVC collapse
    
- Dyspnoea ‚Üí lung sliding
    
- Trauma ‚Üí FAST
    
- Chest pain ‚Üí cardiac function
    

---

## **B. Vascular Access**

- Linear probe
    
- Real-time guidance
    
- In-plane (see whole needle) vs out-of-plane (dot sign)  
    

---

## **C. FAST / eFAST**

Used in trauma to detect **free fluid**.  
4 classical windows:

- RUQ (Morrison pouch)
    
- LUQ
    
- Pelvis
    
- Pericardium  
    

**eFAST adds:**

- Lung windows for:
    
    - **Pneumothorax** (loss of lung sliding)
        
    - **Hemothorax**  
        

---

## **D. Echocardiography**

**TTE** (transthoracic) & **TEE** (transoesophageal)  
Used to assess:

- LVEF
    
- Valve pathology
    
- Pericardial effusion
    
- Wall motion abnormalities  
    

---

## **E. Joint aspiration & injection**

- Linear probe
    
- Ensures accurate needle entry  
    

---

## **F. Breast ultrasound**

- Characterises mass (solid vs cystic)  
    

---

## **G. Cranial ultrasound (neonatal)**

- Through **fontanelles** (bone blocks US)  
    

---

## **H. Obstetric ultrasound**

- Dating, anomaly screening, Doppler
    
- Must follow TI/MI guidelines strictly  
    

---

## **I. Lithotripsy**

- Uses biphasic ultrasound shock waves to **crush stones**

# üî• **13. ULTRASOUND ‚Äì HIGH-YIELD REVISION TABLE**

### **Probe selection**

|Probe|Frequency|Use|
|---|---|---|
|Linear|High|Vascular, MSK, superficial|
|Curvilinear|Low|Abdomen, FAST|
|Phased array|Low|Cardiac, between ribs|
|Intraluminal|High|EV, ER, TEE|

### **Modes**

- **B-mode** = structural 2D
    
- **Color Doppler** = flow (BART)
    
- **PW Doppler** = velocities
    
- **M-mode** = moving structures
    
- **3D** = OB, mass evaluation
    

### **Artefacts**

- Shadowing ‚Üí bone/stone
    
- Enhancement ‚Üí fluid
    
- Anisotropy ‚Üí tendons
    

### **Safety**

- TI < 1, MI < 1
    
- ALARA
    
- Avoid Doppler early pregnancy
    

### **FAST**

- Morrison pouch
    
- Perisplenic
    
- Pelvis
    
- Pericardial
    
- Add lungs for eFAST
