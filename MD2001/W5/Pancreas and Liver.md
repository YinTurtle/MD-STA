### **Bicarbonate Secretion**

- Secretin (increases cAMP levels) and Ach (increases Ca levels) stimulate duct cell secretion
- Cl$^-$/HCO$_3$$^-$ exchanger (SLC26 family) and CFTR chloride channel important for apical secretion of HCO$_3$$^-$
- Substance P inhibits HCO$_3$$^-$ secretion
![[Pasted image 20251016033359.png]]

---

#### **Exocrine Pancreatic Regulation**

- Recall meal triggers 3 phases:

- Cephalic phase
	- sight, smell, taste, mastication (process of chewing food)
	- vagal pathways
	- 25% maximum enzyme secretion
- Gastric phase
	- distention, gastrin?
	- vagal-cholinergic
	- 10-20% maximum enzyme secretion
- Intestinal phase
	- AAs, FAs stimuate I cells in duodenum to release CCK
	- Also stimulates a vagovagal enteropancreatic reflex that stimulates acinar cells
	- H+ stimulate S cells in duodenum to release secretin
	- 50-80% maximum enzyme secretion
![[Pasted image 20251016033715.png|500]]

#### *Pancreatic Clinical Conditions*

- Cystic fibrosis
	- autosomal recessive disease
	- progressive pancreatic and pulmonary insufficiency
	- mutations in CFTR gene which codes for cAMP-regulated chloride channel
	- leads to decrease in HCO$_3$$^-$ secretion, blockage of ducts and eventual destruction of the pancreas
	- Lack of digestive enzymes leads to maldigestion of nutrients
- Acute pancreatitis
	- inflammatory condition that may cause local damage to pancreas
	- most common cause
		- alcohol
		- gallstones
	- pathogenesis thought to originate in pancreatic acinar cells

## üß† **The Pancreas**

### **1. Overview & Relations**

- Lies **retroperitoneally**, tucked behind the stomach.
    
- Its **head** sits in the **C-shaped duodenum**, **tail** reaches toward the spleen.
    
- **Portal vein** forms behind the neck of pancreas (level **L1**).

|Component|Function|Output|
|---|---|---|

|**Duct cells**|Exocrine|Bicarbonate-rich fluid (neutralises acid)|

- Most of it drains via **main pancreatic duct**, which joins the **common bile duct** at the **ampulla of Vater**, controlled by the **sphincter of Oddi**.

### **4. Exocrine Pancreas Functions**

- **Neutralise acid** coming from stomach.
    
- **Digest macronutrients** (proteins, carbs, fats).

## üèóÔ∏è **The Liver**

### **1. Overview & Structure**

- Largest internal organ (1.2‚Äì1.5 kg; ~2% body weight)
    
- Right, left, **quadrate** (near gallbladder) and **caudate** (near IVC) lobes
    
- Covered by peritoneum except **bare area**
    

üß© **Diagram to Include:**

- Labeled lobes + falciform ligament + hepatic triad + bile canaliculi.
    

---

### **2. Microscopic Structure**

- **Lobules**: hexagonal units centred on a **central vein**
    
- Each corner has a **portal triad**:
    
    - **Hepatic artery**
        
    - **Portal vein**
        
    - **Bile duct**
        

‚û°Ô∏è **Blood flow:** from portal triad ‚Üí sinusoids ‚Üí central vein  
‚û°Ô∏è **Bile flow:** opposite direction, from hepatocytes ‚Üí canaliculi ‚Üí bile ducts

üß† **Analogy:**  
Imagine each lobule as a _honeycomb cell_ ‚Äî blood flows inward like nectar, bile flows outward like honey through its own channels.

---

### **3. Blood Supply**

|Vessel|% of Blood|Function|
|---|---|---|
|**Portal vein**|~75%|Nutrient-rich, deoxygenated blood from GI tract|
|**Hepatic artery**|~25%|Oxygenated blood from aorta|
|**Peribiliary plexus**|Capillary network modifying bile composition||

### **4. Liver Functions**

#### üß¨ Metabolism

- **Carbohydrates:** gluconeogenesis, glycogenolysis, glycogenesis
    
- **Lipids:** lipogenesis, lipolysis
    
- **Proteins:** deamination, urea formation
    

#### üíß Bile Production

- **Components:** bile acids, bilirubin, cholesterol, phospholipids, water, electrolytes
    
- **Purpose:** emulsify fats ‚Üí ‚Üë surface area for enzyme action.
    

#### üßΩ Detoxification

- Ammonia ‚Üí urea
    
- Ethanol & drugs ‚Üí metabolised (via CYP450 enzymes)
    

#### ü©∏ Coagulation

- Produces **fibrinogen**, **prothrombin**, other factors
    

#### üõ°Ô∏è Immunity

- **Kupffer cells** remove bacteria from portal blood

## ü´ô **The Gallbladder**

### **1. Structure & Function**

- Stores and **concentrates bile**.
    
- Has a **spiral valve (of Heister)** controlling flow.
    
- **Sphincter of Oddi** controls bile entry into duodenum.
    

|State|Sphincter|Bile Flow|
|---|---|---|
|**Fasting**|Contracted|‚Üí Gallbladder|
|**Post-meal**|Relaxed (via CCK)|‚Üí Duodenum|

---

### **2. Regulation**

|Phase|Trigger|Mediator|Action|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Cephalic**|Sight/smell/taste|Vagal|Mild bile secretion|
|**Gastric**|Distension|Vagal|Moderate bile flow|
|**Intestinal**|Amino/Fatty acids|**CCK**, **Secretin**|GB contraction + sphincter relaxation + HCO‚ÇÉ‚Åª secretion|

üß† **Analogy:**  
The gallbladder is like a **storage tank** that releases bile when the ‚Äúfat alarm‚Äù (CCK) goes off.

---

### **3. Clinical Conditions**

|Condition|Mechanism|Features|
|---|---|---|
|**Cholestasis**|Blocked bile flow|Jaundice, itching, hepatocyte injury|
|**Gallstones (Cholelithiasis)**|Cholesterol supersaturation|Obstruction, pain, possible pancreatitis|
