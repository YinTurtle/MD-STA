## 2Ô∏è‚É£ Overview of Circulation

Think of the vascular system as a **hierarchical network of pipes**:

1. **Elastic arteries** ‚Üí **Muscular arteries** ‚Üí **Arterioles**
    
2. ‚Üí **Capillaries** ‚Üí **Venules** ‚Üí **Veins** ‚Üí **Heart**
    
3. The **lymphatic system** runs alongside, collecting leaked fluid and returning it to venous circulation.
    

---

## 3Ô∏è‚É£ General Structure of Blood Vessels

All blood vessels share the same basic wall layers, or _tunics_:

|Layer|Composition|Function|
|---|---|---|
|**Tunica intima (interna)**|Endothelium + basement membrane|Smooth lining for blood flow; secretes vasoactive substances|
|**Tunica media**|Smooth muscle + elastic fibres|Regulates diameter (vasoconstriction/vasodilation); maintains BP|
|**Tunica externa (adventitia)**|Connective tissue (collagen & elastin)|Structural support; anchors to surroundings; contains vasa vasorum & nerves|

---

### üîπ _Histology Tip for Exams:_

- Arteries have **thicker tunica media** (more muscle + elastic fibres).
    
- Veins have **thicker tunica externa** and **larger lumens**.
    
- Capillaries = only **endothelium + basement membrane**.
    

---

## 4Ô∏è‚É£ Tunica Intima (Inner Layer)

- **Endothelium:** simple squamous epithelium lining lumen
    
- **Basement membrane:** supports endothelium
    
- **Internal elastic lamina:** found in large arteries ‚Üí allows stretch and recoil
    

**Clinical relevance:**  
Endothelial dysfunction (e.g. from hypertension or smoking) ‚Üí atherosclerosis ‚Üí plaque formation ‚Üí vessel narrowing.

---

## 5Ô∏è‚É£ Tunica Media (Middle Layer)

- Circular **smooth muscle** layers
    
- Contains **elastic fibres** (more in arteries)
    
- Controlled by **sympathetic vasomotor nerves**
    
- Allows:
    
    - **Vasoconstriction:** ‚Üì lumen size ‚Üí ‚Üë resistance & BP
        
    - **Vasodilation:** ‚Üë lumen size ‚Üí ‚Üì resistance & BP
        

**Remember:**  
‚û° Arteries = pressure regulators  
‚û° Veins = volume reservoirs

---

## 6Ô∏è‚É£ Tunica Externa (Adventitia)

- **Connective tissue sheath** with collagen & elastin
    
- Thickest in **veins**
    
- Contains **vasa vasorum** ‚Äî small vessels supplying outer walls of large arteries and veins
    

**Mnemonic:** _Vasa vasorum = ‚Äúvessels of the vessels‚Äù_

## 7Ô∏è‚É£ Types of Arteries

### 1. **Elastic Arteries**

- Largest (e.g. aorta, pulmonary trunk, carotids)
    
- Contain **abundant elastic tissue** in all layers
    
- Function as **pressure reservoirs** ‚Äî expand in systole, recoil in diastole
    
- Ensures continuous blood flow between heartbeats
    

ü©∫ _Clinical link:_ Loss of elasticity (ageing/atherosclerosis) ‚Üí widened pulse pressure.

---

### 2. **Muscular Arteries**

- Medium-sized (e.g. femoral, brachial, radial)
    
- Thick tunica media ‚Üí major role in **vasoconstriction/dilation**
    
- Regulate **regional blood flow**
    
- Involved in **collateral circulation** (arterial anastomoses)
    

**Anastomoses:** Natural interconnections between arteries ‚Äî ensure blood supply even if one route is blocked (important around joints).

---

### 3. **Arterioles**

- Smallest arteries (0.3 mm ‚Üí 10 ¬µm)
    
- Regulate flow into capillary beds via **precapillary sphincters**
    
- Major determinant of **peripheral resistance** and **blood pressure**
    

**Clinical relevance:**  
Target of many antihypertensive drugs (e.g. calcium-channel blockers act on arteriolar smooth muscle).

---

## 8Ô∏è‚É£ Capillaries ‚Äî The Exchange Network

- Diameter ~7‚Äì9 ¬µm (just wide enough for one RBC)
    
- Wall = single layer of **endothelium + basal lamina**
    
- Site of exchange between **blood and tissues** via:
    
    - Diffusion (O‚ÇÇ, CO‚ÇÇ)
        
    - Osmosis
        
    - Filtration
        
    - Active transport
        

### ü©∏ Types of Capillaries

|Type|Structure|Function|Found In|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Continuous**|Tight endothelium|Least permeable|Muscle, skin, CNS, lungs|
|**Fenestrated**|Pores in endothelium|Filter & absorb|Kidneys, small intestine, glands|
|**Sinusoidal**|Large gaps & basement membrane discontinuous|Allows passage of cells|Liver, spleen, bone marrow|

## 9Ô∏è‚É£ Venules and Veins

### Venules:

- Formed by union of capillaries
    
- Very porous ‚Äî allow **white cell migration** (diapedesis)
    
- Lead into veins
    

### Veins:

- **Thin walls, large lumens** ‚Üí low pressure system
    
- **Valves (intimal folds)** prevent backflow
    
- Contain ~65% of total blood volume ‚Üí **capacitance vessels**
    

**Mechanisms aiding venous return:**

1. **Muscular pump** ‚Äî skeletal muscle contraction squeezes veins
    
2. **Respiratory pump** ‚Äî thoracic pressure changes during breathing
    
3. **Venae comitantes** ‚Äî paired veins accompanying arteries; arterial pulsation helps flow
    

---

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Clinical Correlations

- **Valve incompetence** ‚Üí varicose veins
    
- **Venous thrombosis (DVT):** stasis, hypercoagulability, endothelial injury (Virchow‚Äôs triad)

## 11Ô∏è‚É£ The Lymphatic System

### Functions:

1. **Returns excess interstitial fluid** ‚Üí bloodstream
    
2. **Transports dietary fats** (from intestines via lacteals)
    
3. **Provides immune defense** (lymphocytes, antigen screening)
    

**Key components:**

- **Lymph capillaries:** very permeable, absorb tissue fluid ‚Üí lymph
    
- **Lymph vessels:** similar to veins but thinner; have valves
    
- **Lymph nodes:** filter lymph; contain immune cells
    
- **Lymphoid organs:** spleen, thymus, tonsils, Peyer‚Äôs patches
    

---

### Main Lymphatic Pathways

|Duct|Drains|Opens Into|
|---|---|---|
|**Thoracic duct**|Entire body except right upper quadrant|Left subclavian & internal jugular junction|
|**Right lymphatic duct**|Right side of head, thorax, & right upper limb|Right subclavian & internal jugular junction|

---

### Lymph Node Clusters (Clinical Importance)

|Cluster|Drains|Clinical Relevance|
|---|---|---|
|**Cervical**|Head & neck|Infections, metastasis from oral cancers|
|**Axillary**|Upper limb, breast, upper thoracic wall|Breast cancer spread|
|**Inguinal**|Lower limb, external genitalia, lower abdomen|STI or lower limb infection tracking|

üß† _Mnemonic:_ ‚Äú**C-A-I** ‚Äî Cervical, Axillary, Inguinal‚Äù = common palpable nodes.

## 12Ô∏è‚É£ Summary Table: Vessel Comparison

|Feature|Arteries|Veins|Lymphatics|
|---|---|---|---|
|Pressure|High|Low|Very low|
|Wall thickness|Thick (muscular)|Thin|Very thin|
|Valves|No (except semilunar)|Yes|Yes|

|Tunica externa|Thin|Thickest|Thin|
|Lumen size|Narrow|Wide|Variable|
|Flow direction|From heart|To heart|To venous system|
